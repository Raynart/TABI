The JR Pass: Who It's For, Who It Isn't, and How to Not Overpay

The JR Pass is one of Japan's most famous travel products. It's also, for a lot of itineraries, a waste of money.

In this article

  1. The cost
  2. Break-even: when the pass wins
  3. Nozomi vs Hikari
  4. What the pass covers that people miss
  5. Alternatives to the JR Pass
  6. If you have decided to buy one

The cost

The 7-day JR Pass costs ¥50,000 (around $330 USD at current rates). For that price you get unlimited travel on most JR-operated trains, including the Shinkansen, for one week. The 14-day version is ¥80,000. If your itinerary involves Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and Hiroshima in that sequence, the math works. If your itinerary is mostly in one city with a few day trips, it almost certainly doesn't.

Break-even: when the pass wins

The break-even calculation for the 7-day pass: a single Tokyo–Kyoto Shinkansen (Hikari) ticket is ¥13,870 each way. Two of those is ¥27,740. Add Kyoto–Osaka (¥1,420), Osaka–Hiroshima (¥5,720 each way), a couple of JR local trips, and you're somewhere near ¥60,000. The pass wins. If you're only doing Tokyo–Kyoto round-trip with no other long-distance travel, a single round-trip costs ¥27,740 against the pass price of ¥50,000. You lose ¥22,260.

Nozomi vs Hikari

The JR Pass does not cover the Nozomi or Mizuho (the fastest Shinkansen), only the Hikari and Kodama. Tokyo to Kyoto on a Hikari takes about 2h45m versus 2h15m on a Nozomi. This is not a big difference and most people don't notice it.

What the pass covers that people miss

What the pass does cover that people underestimate: the Narita Express (N'EX) from the airport, the local JR lines within Tokyo (which serve some neighborhoods that subway doesn't), the JR Haruka from Kansai Airport into Kyoto, and most Shinkansen within Kyushu (on the Kyushu JR Pass). If you're starting from Narita, the N'EX alone is ¥3,070 to Shinjuku. Over seven days that's two trips or ¥6,140 saved before you board a Shinkansen.

Alternatives to the JR Pass

Alternatives to consider: IC cards (Suica) cover everything within cities. Individual Shinkansen tickets bought in advance can be slightly discounted through JR's Ekinet system. The Osaka Amazing Pass and the Kyoto Sightseeing Card cover local transit within those cities and don't require a JR Pass to function alongside them.

If you have decided to buy one

If you've already decided to buy the pass: buy it before leaving your home country. It used to be only available abroad, and while you can now buy it in Japan, the domestic price is roughly 10–20% higher. Get it from JR's official website or an authorized travel agent in your country. Activate it at a JR office in any major station, not at the airport machines.
