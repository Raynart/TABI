Golden Week: What It Is, Why It Will Destroy Your Trip, and When to Go Instead

Golden Week runs from late April to early May. If your Japan trip overlaps with it, you have two options: embrace the chaos or reschedule.

In this article

  1. What Golden Week is
  2. What actually happens
  3. If you are already booked
  4. Shinkansen strategy
  5. When to go instead

What Golden Week is

Golden Week is Japan's largest consecutive holiday period 窶・a cluster of four national holidays between April 29 and May 5 that most companies treat as a full week off. Around 20 million Japanese people travel domestically during this window. International visitors add to this. The result is that every popular destination, every Shinkansen, every tourist attraction, and every hotel in Japan is at or near maximum capacity simultaneously.

What actually happens

What actually happens: the Tokaido Shinkansen from Tokyo to Osaka sells out weeks in advance and runs extra services that also sell out. Popular ryokan are fully booked four to six months ahead. The queue for Fushimi Inari in Kyoto, normally manageable at 7am, is significant by 8am. Hakone accommodation triples in price. Narita arrivals and departures are maximally busy. None of this is impossible to navigate; it's just not the Japan trip most people imagine.

If you are already booked

If you're already booked during Golden Week: lean into it. Go to places that are interesting specifically when full of Japanese people rather than tourists. City parks during Golden Week 窶・Yoyogi in Tokyo, Maruyama in Kyoto 窶・are excellent: families with picnic setups, food stalls, outdoor performances, a festive atmosphere that you don't get in October. The experience is genuinely different rather than categorically worse.

Shinkansen strategy

The Shinkansen strategy: if you have a JR Pass, reserved seats on Shinkansen are essential. Reserve them the moment you activate your pass. Non-reserved cars during Golden Week are standing-room only from Tokyo to Kyoto. If you don't have a JR Pass, buy Shinkansen tickets as far in advance as possible through Ekinet or at any JR ticket office.

When to go instead

When to go instead: late October and early November for autumn color with reasonable crowds. January for minimum tourists and maximum winter onsen value. Late September, after the summer humidity breaks. The weeks immediately after Golden Week ends (May 10窶・5) are excellent 窶・the weather is mild, crowds have dispersed, and hotel rates drop back to normal.
