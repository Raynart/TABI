Japanese Stationery Is Worth the Suitcase Space

Pens, notebooks, tape, paper clips. Japan has turned the mundane into an argument for why other countries aren't trying hard enough.

The Pilot Hi-Tec-C case for Japanese stationery

The Pilot Hi-Tec-C is a 0.3mm gel pen that costs ﾂ･220 at any stationery shop in Japan and writes with a precision that no comparable pen in Europe or North America has matched at any price. This is not enthusiasm. It is a factual statement about the difference between the Japanese stationery industry and everyone else's, and it is the reason you should set aside half a day for stationery shopping.

The main chains

The two main chains: Itoya in Ginza (12 floors, a serious institutional stationery store that's been operating since 1904) and Loft (department store format, wide selection, multiple Tokyo locations). Itoya is for people who want to be deliberate about what they buy and spend two hours doing it. Loft is for people who want variety and efficiency. Tokyu Hands, now rebranded as Hands, has the broadest range of craft and stationery supplies.

Hobonichi planners and Midori notebooks

What to buy. Hobonichi planners: a cult Japanese planner published by the company that made Mother 2. Available in several formats (Techo, Weeks, Cousin); the standard Techo is an A6 daily planner printed on Tomoe River paper, which is 52gsm and shows through ink without bleeding. ﾂ･3,300 at official stores. Midori MD paper products: notebooks and pads using MD paper, which accepts fountain pen ink unusually well. The A5 blank notebook is ﾂ･900.

Pens worth buying

For pens: the Uni-ball One (gel ink, available in 0.38mm and 0.5mm, multiple colors, ﾂ･300) and the Zebra Mildliner (a dual-tip highlighter with pastel tones that don't cause eye fatigue) are the two most commonly recommended items by people who work with paper professionally. Both are widely available and inexpensive enough to buy in quantity.

The suitcase argument

Suitcase space: stationery is light and flat. A full day's shopping fits in a document bag. The Pilot Hi-Tec-C pens are ﾂ･220 each and weigh nothing. The Hobonichi is the same size as a passport. This is one of the gift categories where there's no excuse not to buy for everyone you know.
